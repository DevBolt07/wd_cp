Language Hub

Explore the diverse linguistic landscape of India

India is home to 22 officially recognized languages and hundreds of regional dialects. Language is not just a tool for communication but a medium of culture, identity, and tradition. Dive into resources, fun facts, and learning guides for the most spoken Indian languages.

Hindi

Indo-Aryan

Most spoken language in India. Learn script, grammar & common phrases.

Bengali

Indo-Aryan

Language of Tagore. Rich literary tradition and unique script.

Tamil

Dravidian

One of the oldest classical languages in the world. Still actively used.

Marathi

Indo-Aryan

Spoken widely in Maharashtra. Rich in folk literature and poetry.

Telugu

Dravidian

Known for its musical phonetics and vibrant cinema.

Gujarati

Indo-Aryan

Language of Mahatma Gandhi. Known for business and folk tales.

Kannada

Dravidian

Classical language of Karnataka with a strong literary legacy.

Punjabi

Indo-Aryan

Widely spoken in India and abroad. Language of Bhangra and Gurubani.

Malayalam

Dravidian

Spoken in Kerala. Complex script and rich cinema/literature.

Assamese

Spoken in Assam. Known for lyrical flow and literature.

Urdu

Rich poetic heritage and elegant script.

Odia

Spoken in Odisha. Known for devotional literature.

Sanskrit

Ancient language of India. Foundation of many Indian languages.

Konkani

Primarily spoken in Goa. Rich in folk traditions.

Manipuri

Also called Meitei. Spoken in Manipur. Has own script.

Bodo

Spoken by Bodo people in Northeast India. Tibeto-Burman family.

Santhali

Spoken by Santhal tribes. Uses Ol Chiki script.

Dogri

Spoken in Jammu region. Rich oral tradition.

Kashmiri

Dardic language spoken in Kashmir Valley.

Maithili

Spoken in Bihar. Known for Vidyapati's poetry.
